Busy Light Help

Getting Started

Busy Light connects to a Home Assistant instance on your network to activate scenes. Before you can use the app you need a Home Assistant server URL and a long-lived access token.

What you need

  • A running Home Assistant instance reachable from your Mac.
  • At least one scene configured in Home Assistant.
  • A long-lived access token for authentication.

Create a long-lived access token

  1. Open your Home Assistant web interface.
  2. Click your profile icon in the bottom-left corner of the sidebar.
  3. Scroll down to the Long-Lived Access Tokens section.
  4. Click Create Token, give it a name (e.g. "Busy Light"), and click OK.
  5. Copy the token immediately — it is only shown once.

Configure the connection

  1. Click the Busy Light icon in the menu bar and choose Settings…
  2. In the Home Assistant tab, enter your server URL (e.g. http://homeassistant.local:8123).
  3. Paste your long-lived access token into the Token field.
  4. Click Test Connection to verify everything works. A green checkmark means you are connected.
Your access token is stored securely in the macOS Keychain and is never sent anywhere other than your Home Assistant server.

Next steps

Once connected, head over to Adding Scenes to choose which scenes appear in the menu bar.
